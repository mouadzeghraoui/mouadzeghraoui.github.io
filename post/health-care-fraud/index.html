The Problem statement :

A couple of months ago, I read an article about the impact of healthcare Fraud on the healthcare system, and I realized that this is a crime that has a huge impact on the healthcare system and on us as an individual. I really invite you to read this article.

And I was thinking how can we help insurance companies to detect this kind of fraud, and save Time and Money.

And I realized that Data Science is key for Insurance company to Detect Potential HealthCare fraud.

What is a Health care fraud ?

Health care fraud is a crime that involves misrepresenting information, concealing information, or deceiving a person or entity in order to receive benefits, or to make a financial profit. Both individuals and healthcare providers commit health care fraud, in a number of different ways.

According to the National Health Care Anti-Fraud Association (NHCAA), the financial lose due to healthcare fraud is more than $100 billion every year, and have a lot of consequences such as:

  • Reduce benefits & coverage
  • Increased cost of providing insurance benefits

Healthcare Fraud = submission of fraudulent claims by medical facilities to steal money from insurance companies.

Fraud claims come in different forms such as:

  • Using insurance that belongs to someone else
  • Failing to remove someone no longer eligible from a policy
  • Providing false information in order to receive medical coverage
  • Duplicating claims for the same or different patients
  • Providing a service that is not covered under a patient’s insurance policy, then billing for a service that is covered

Source :

  • https://www.nhcaa.org/resources/health-care-anti-fraud-resources/the-challenge-of-health-care-fraud/
  • https://legaldictionary.net/health-care-fraud/

Why would detecting these kind of fraud be helpful?

By detecting potential fraud using our Historical Data, we can really save a huge time and effort to the insurance companies, and also to understand the behavior of potential fraud providers, and discover the important parameters that can help us to to that, and finnally to build a predictive model that can flag the potential fraud providers based on the claims fields

Data Collection

The Dataset that I used in this Project is a real Dataset,That I found on Kaggle.

Like all Machine Learning projects, it begins and ends with the data.

We have 4 Datasets:

  • Inpatient Data : This data provides insights about the claims filed for those patients who are admitted in the hospitals. It also provides additional details like their admission and discharge dates and admit d diagnosis code.
  • Outpatient Data: This data provides details about the claims filed for those patients who visit hospitals and not admitted in it.
  • Beneficiary Details Data: This data contains beneficiary details like health conditions,regioregion they belong to etc.
  • The last one Label, Contains the Label of Fraud : Yes or No

So we can conclude that our Dataset includes information about:

  • Beneficiary like Age, Race, Country, State, Gender
  • Inpatient/outpatient claims informations such as the amount, Medical codes, Physician ID
  • Label (Fraud or Not Fraud)

From the Data Report we can see that we have some features with 100% of Missing Data, and some features with high cardinality.

  • I removed all Features with 100% of Missing Data

After that I ran some initial Data Analysis, to understand the relationship between our features, and between our features and our target variable "PotentialFraud".

In this part, I tried to answer a set of questions such as:

  • The Number of Beneficiary in the DataSet?
  • The Number and Type of Doctors in the DataSet?
  • Our Doctors serves different provider/Hospital?
  • Our Benefeciary goes to different Hospital?
  • Inpatient has worse health conditions than the Outpatient?
  • Beneficiaries who visit the hospital more often have worse health conditions than the others?
  • The relationship between the patient ages and the counts of medical claims?
  • The relationship between Patient's age and their chronic conditions?
  • Our Providers have Duplicated Claims?

And many more, all these questions help us to understand our Data, and to understand the fraudsters' behaviour.
